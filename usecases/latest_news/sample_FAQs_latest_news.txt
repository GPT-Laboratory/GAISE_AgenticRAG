SAMPLE FAQs — LATEST NEWS USE CASE (Multilingual News Articles & Event Tracking)
================================================================================

Purpose
-------
This file provides sample questions for participants building Agentic RAG applications on top of the latest-news document corpus in ./data/. The corpus contains news articles collected from many international sources and includes metadata such as title, link, keywords/categories, creator/author, description, content/full description, publication date, image URL, and source ID.

The dataset is suitable for testing news-oriented retrieval tasks such as article lookup, source-aware answering, timeline reconstruction, event comparison, topic clustering, cross-lingual retrieval, and multi-source summarization.

NOTE — MULTILINGUAL AND TIME-SENSITIVE CORPUS
---------------------------------------------
The documents are multilingual and come from a wide range of sources. Many articles are not in English, so cross-lingual retrieval is part of the challenge: a question asked in English may need to retrieve relevant Italian, Thai, Spanish, Arabic, German, Russian, French, Hindi, or other-language articles.

The corpus is also time-sensitive. Publication date, source identity, duplicate stories, and near-duplicate syndicated articles matter. A good Agentic RAG system should not only retrieve relevant articles, but also compare timestamps, identify repeated coverage, and cite claims back to the correct source article.

How to use these questions
--------------------------
The questions are split into two groups so you can test both retrieval modes your agent must handle:
  • SINGLE-DOCUMENT — the answer can be found within one article or one metadata record. Tests precise retrieval, metadata extraction, and grounded article-level answering.
  • MULTI-DOCUMENT — the answer requires retrieving several related articles and then comparing, aggregating, deduplicating, or synthesizing across sources. These are the questions that test whether the system is truly agentic rather than a simple top-k lookup.

================================================================================
PART A — SINGLE-DOCUMENT QUESTIONS  (answer found within one article)
================================================================================
1. What is the title, source, publication date, and short description of the article about Apple One Premier launching in Italy?
2. What happened to Luciano Spalletti after the Roma-Napoli match, and which source reported it?
3. Which article discusses the top Thai provinces with the highest COVID-19 cases, and what key numbers are mentioned?
4. What does the article about Dapper Labs and NBA Top Shot say about professional sports leagues and NFTs?
5. Which article reports on U.S. stock-market highs and low volatility in the Forex market, and what explanation is given?
6. What information is provided about Napoli-Bologna and possible fan-zone arrangements?
7. Which article discusses Juventus player Paulo Dybala, and why is he described as important for Allegri?
8. What does the dataset record say about the boxing athletes Worapoj Petchkoom and Suriya Prasathinphimai?
9. Which article is linked to the keyword/category “Coronavirus,” and what is its main topic?
10. What source, title, and publication time are associated with a selected Premier League article?
11. Which article includes an image URL for Luciano Spalletti, and what is the article about?
12. What creator or author is listed for the iPhoneItalia article about Apple One Premier?
13. Which article reports on football or Serie A, and what is the main claim in its description?
14. What is the full description or content field for a selected article, and how does it differ from the short description?
15. What keywords/categories are assigned to a selected business or finance article?
16. What original news link is stored for a selected article, and which source ID does it belong to?

================================================================================
PART B — MULTI-DOCUMENT QUESTIONS  (retrieve + synthesize across articles)
================================================================================
1. Build a timeline of news articles published between 2021-10-24 and 2021-10-26 about COVID-19. Which countries or regions are covered, and how do the reported situations differ?
2. Compare how different sources covered football news on 2021-10-26. Which teams, players, leagues, or managers appear most often?
3. Summarize the main business and finance stories in the corpus. Cover stock markets, Forex, banking/financial services, and economy-related articles.
4. Identify and compare articles about technology topics such as Apple services, NFTs, gaming, and computer/electronics news. What themes appear across sources?
5. Find duplicate or near-duplicate stories across different sources. Which articles appear to report the same event, and how do their titles or descriptions differ?
6. Compare coverage of sports beyond football, such as NBA, Formula 1, boxing, and other national sports stories. What events or personalities are mentioned?
7. Create a source profile for the dataset: which source IDs publish the most articles, what topics do they focus on, and which languages do they appear to use?
8. Analyze the use of keywords/categories across the corpus. Which major topic groups appear, and how consistently are they assigned across sources?
9. For a selected breaking-news topic, retrieve articles from at least three sources and compare publication times, headlines, and key facts.
10. Summarize entertainment and lifestyle news in the corpus. Which celebrities, TV, culture, or lifestyle themes appear across multiple articles?
11. Compare political news coverage across languages and regions. Which countries or political topics are represented, and what source diversity exists?
12. Build an event-centered summary for one major topic, such as football, COVID-19, finance, or technology. Decompose the topic into sub-questions, retrieve relevant articles, remove duplicates, and produce a cited multi-source answer.

================================================================================
TIP FOR PARTICIPANTS
--------------------
The multi-document questions are deliberately the hard cases. A naive top-k RAG system may retrieve several articles with similar keywords but miss source diversity, chronology, language variation, or duplicated syndicated coverage. Use your agent to (a) decompose the question into sub-queries, (b) retrieve iteratively across languages and sources, (c) check publication dates and source IDs, (d) group duplicates and near-duplicates, and (e) cite each claim back to the article record it came from.
